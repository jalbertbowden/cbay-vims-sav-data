Metadata for Chesapeake Bay Tier I Coverage

These metadata describe the Chesapeake Bay Tier I coverage dataset that
was produced at the Virginia Institute of Marine Science by combining
data from twelve annual submerged aquatic vegetation distribution and
abundance surveys, covering a period from 1971 to 1990.

    Description:
      Abstract:
	The Chesapeake Bay Tier I Coverage was produced by combining
	all SAV polygons from the 1971, 1974, 1978, 1979, 1980, 1981,
	1984, 1985, 1986, 1987, 1989, and 1990 annual submerged aquatic
	vegetation distribution and abundance surveys which were
	compiled from aerial photography.
      Purpose:
	The Tier I coverage represents the first tier of the
	three-tiered strategy that has been adopted to evaluate
	Chesapeake Bay restoration efforts.  It includes all areas that
	were shown to support SAV by the annual surveys for the period
	of 1971 to 1990.

Data_Quality_Information

    Attribute_Accuracy:
      Attribute_Accuracy_Report:
        The density attribute has been checked by comparison with the source 
        material
    Logical_Consistency_Report:
      The coverage contains full Arc/Info polygon topology.  There are no
      unlabeled polygons, no intersections, no unattached lines and all lines
      have both left and right polygons identified.
    Completeness_Report:
      The dataset contains all SAV areas that were identified from the
      areas flown.  Some areas that are presumed to contain no SAV were
      not flown.  Some small beds, particularly along narrow
      tributaries may not have been distinguishable on the aerial
      photography.  Some annual surveys did not cover the entire bay.
    Positional_Accuracy:
      Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy:
        Horizontal_Positional_Accuracy_Report:
	  Horizontal positional accuracy is based upon the use of USGS
	  source quadrangles which are compiled to meet National Map
	  Accuracy Standards (NMAS).  The aerial photographs were
	  manually overlaid on USGS base material during the photo
	  interpretation process.  The maps were then digitized within
	  24 meters of the interperted line.

      Process_Step:
        Process_Description:
	  The coverage for each year was dissolved, retaining only the
	  density attribute which was renamed d71, d74, ... ,  d90.
	  These were then unioned to form a single large coverage.
	  It was inspected to determine the area contributed solely
	  by any one year and to detect any other anomallies.  Finally
	  a tier1 item was added to the coverage and used to code SAV
	  polygons as one and everything else as zero.  The coverage
	  was then disolved on the tier1 item and unioned with the
	  segment and quadrangle coverages to form the Tier I
	  coverage.
        Source_Used_Citation_Abbreviation: 1971_SAV, 1974_SAV, 1978_SAV,
